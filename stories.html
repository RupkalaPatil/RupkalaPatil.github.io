The stories behind the résumé.

A CV lists what happened. These short stories are about how it actually went — the messy middles, the fixes we didn't wait for, and what each chapter taught me about making data trustworthy.

01 Customer data platform · 21+ brands

Define "one customer" first

When I joined the Customer Data Platform program for a major GCC automotive group, the brief sounded simple: unify the customer. In practice, the hardest part came before any technology — agreeing how you know two records are the same person. A mobile number and an email? A national ID and a name? Across 21+ brands, everyone had their own instinct.

Freezing eleven matching rules took longer than building anything. That was the real lesson: the bottleneck in customer unification isn't code — it's getting the business to trust one shared definition of "this is one customer."

02 Databricks · Python · SQL

The fix we didn't wait for

We were one of the earliest adopters of the CDP product, and early adoption came with a critical surprise: incoming data wasn't merging against the unification ID. Load the same customer a month later and, instead of updating their profile, the platform quietly created a duplicate. The vendor fix was months away.

We didn't wait. We moved unification onto our own data platform in Databricks — built a Customer Master View pulling every customer attribute into one coherent surface, and wrote the matching logic ourselves in Python and SQL. Three months later we had roughly ninety-five percent match accuracy, owned in-house rather than waited on from a patch.

03 SAP C4C · IS-AUTO · Emarsys

Halfway migrations are the hardest

In parallel, a new CRM was replacing a custom branch app for the lead-to-sales-order journey — but only halfway. The front end moved cleanly, while customer creation stayed in the legacy automotive ERP, which the new CRM couldn't connect to directly. Every connector had to be designed, justified, and budgeted deliberately.

With marketing migrating platforms at the same time, we phased the rollout: smaller brands first, proving the architecture end to end before the biggest brands followed. Sequencing, not heroics, is what made it land.

04 KPI governance · Power BI

Pulling reporting back to the center

Moving into data science & data engineering operations leadership, the mandate shifted from building one platform to governing an entire reporting ecosystem. Every brand had grown its own reports, its own definitions, its own version of the truth.

We ran workshops across Sales, After-Sales, Parts, and Service until we had one governed set of KPI definitions — then consolidated reporting to the center while migrating off legacy BI and Tableau onto Power BI. Governance sounds dry until you watch executives stop debating whose number is right and start debating what to do about it.

05 Customer 360 · CLTV · Genie

From data foundation to AI

With Customer 360 and Vehicle 360 in place as the real data foundation, we pushed further: customer lifetime value scoring, churn-risk segmentation, and Next Best Offer models built on top of the unified views.

Then we put Databricks Genie over the entire workspace, so business users could ask questions of that data in natural language instead of waiting on a fixed dashboard. AI adoption was only possible because the data underneath had already earned trust.

06 Sentiment analysis · Lost leads

Closing the loop, end to end

The call center was already converting recordings to text and running sentiment analysis — but the calls weren't tied to a lead ID, so all that signal floated disconnected from the customer journey. We rebuilt that linkage as its own project.

That single fix unlocked two more: a Lost Leads analysis, and repairing a long-broken test-drive flow where booking forms lived outside the lead record entirely. We integrated it into the same lead process — and for the first time, the journey was complete, end to end.

Every chapter was the same skill at a different altitude: getting fragmented systems — and fragmented stakeholders — to agree on one trustworthy version of the truth.
